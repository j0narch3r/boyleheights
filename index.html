1973.

Valentina Tamayo is a 20-year-old Mexican immigrant living the American dream. Her husband, Daniel, works in an East L.A. factory while she cleans a house in Beverly Hills. They have a small, rented home with a pretty backyard that both are ready to fill with children.

Valentina is nine months pregnant and has just planted a lemon tree sapling out back to grow along with her first child; she plans to do the same with each one that follows.

Troubled labor. A frantic rush to the operating room. The half-conscious signing of a form forced into her hands.

She awakens to discover her baby was stillborn.

And the doctors have sterilized her.

The Team

Diane Weipert

Writer/Director

Diane Weipert is a screenwriter and filmmaker, whose debut SOLO DIOS SABE (ONLY GOD KNOWS) premiered at the World Cinema Competition at Sundance, and starred Diego Luna and Alice Braga. Her directorial debut, NIÑERA, was one of nine short films selected to tour with Lunafest, and has screened at festivals and events around the world. She has worked with Atlas Entertainment, producer Lucas Foster, actors Benjamin Bratt and Michelle Rodriguez, director Adrian Garcia Bogliano, and many others. Her radio story THE LIVING ROOM has inspired works of theater, ballet, paintings, songs, as well as the Oscar-winning short film, THE NEIGHBOR'S WINDOW. She has also taught film and screenwriting courses as a volunteer for the Prison University Project at San Quentin. Weipert came of age in Spain and Latin America, and is bilingual in English and Spanish.

Agustin Calderon

Director of Photography

Agustin Calderon AMC is an award-winning Mexican cinematographer. He works with top Latin American production companies, including Catatonia Films, Terciopelo Films, and Tribu Films, and teaches Cinematography at the Centro de Capacitación Cinematográfica. His work has won several national and international awards, including two Cannes Lions Awards and a Clio Award. He graduated Summa Cum Laude in Cinematography and Directing at the Centro de Capacitación Cinematográfica (CCC) in Mexico City and joined the Mexican Society of Cinematographers in 2004.
